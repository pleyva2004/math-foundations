# Tour: Math Grad

> "I have measure theory + functional analysis."

Skip foundations. Goes straight to the bits modern ML papers use that pure-math folks may not have seen.

## The path

1. [`27-conditional-expectation`](../concepts/27-conditional-expectation/README.md) — as a sub-sigma-algebra projection. You probably know this; refresh notation.
2. [`32-brownian-motion`](../concepts/32-brownian-motion/README.md), [`33-sde`](../concepts/33-sde/README.md), [`34-ito-calculus`](../concepts/34-ito-calculus/README.md) — for diffusion and flow models.
3. [`38-kl-divergence`](../concepts/38-kl-divergence/README.md), [`39-mutual-information`](../concepts/39-mutual-information/README.md) — refresh the information-theoretic vocabulary in the form ML uses.
4. [`40-information-geometry`](../concepts/40-information-geometry/README.md) — Fisher metric on the simplex; the natural-gradient backbone.
5. [`41-optimal-transport`](../concepts/41-optimal-transport/README.md) — Wasserstein distances; the right metric on distributions with disjoint supports.

These five give you the vocabulary to read every Anthropic-relevant ML paper without translation cost.
